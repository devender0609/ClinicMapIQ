Scoring is a planning signal, not a revenue or referral prediction.

Office fit ranks individual offices for practical outreach. The current rule gives points for referral-office match (+30), phone (+16), website (+12), mapped location (+10), stronger public listing, shorter distance, saved status, and selected growth-goal match. High fit means easier to verify/contact; it does not prove the office will refer.

Action tier turns the same data into a next step: Call this week, Verify first, Save for review, Follow-up due, Protect relationship, Competitor/watch, or Do not pursue. Each office card now shows a short “Why” line so the recommendation is transparent.

Planning score is the overall market-readiness score. It adds referral-office volume, contactable records, mapped records, and saved targets, then subtracts similar-clinic pressure. A practical target is 70+, but only when contact readiness is also strong. It may stay the same after repeated searches when the same public records return and no workflow data changes.

Market opportunity separates referral-office volume from similar-clinic pressure. Contact readiness shows whether the team can actually call, verify, or visit offices; target 75+. Outreach execution shows whether saved offices have owners, touches, and follow-ups; target 60–70+ once outreach begins.

Top barriers update from missing phone/website, weak locations, similar clinics, saved-but-not-contacted offices, missing owners, incomplete referral pathway, due follow-ups, and manual barrier tags entered in Manage.
